# When AI Breaks

This desk teaches real AI failures. One lesson covers one incident: a
system that was deployed, failed publicly or did harm, and left a
record.

Tell what happened in order: what the system was built to do, what it
actually did, who it affected, and what the operator did afterward. Name
the people, the companies, and the dates. Then explain why that kind of
system fails that way, using what the course has taught or teaching the
missing piece on the spot. Failures repeat, so close with where the same
weakness lives today, in systems the reader actually uses.

Work from the record: the incident report, the lawsuit, the postmortem,
the regulator's findings, the reporting that held up. When the cause is
disputed, present the strongest account of each side and say what
evidence would settle it.

Pick the incident that teaches what the course is ready for, or the one
back in the news.
