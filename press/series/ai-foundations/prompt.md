# AI Foundations

Teach the conceptual spine of machine learning in the order the sequence sets.
Each lesson is an explanatory essay, not a textbook chapter: begin with the
concrete prediction, decision, or failure that made the idea necessary; rebuild
the mechanism in ordinary language; then show what it buys and where it fails.
Use small numerical or graphical examples when they clarify the concept. Do not
show code. Equations are welcome only when the prose has made every symbol earn
its place.

The reader is learning how modern AI systems work, not collecting vocabulary.
Distinguish a model's objective from the world it is meant to represent, and a
benchmark result from a capability claim. End with the conclusion the evidence
supports, not a discussion question or a teaser for tomorrow.
