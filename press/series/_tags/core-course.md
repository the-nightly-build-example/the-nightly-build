# Core Course

This is one desk in a synchronized course. The six articles dated the same day
form an edition, read in this order: AI Foundations, Mathematical Intuition,
History of AI, Transformers from First Principles, Canon Papers, and AI in the
World. The order is for the reader, not a license to depend on an unpublished
same-night article. Make each article stand on its own while spending concepts
already established in earlier days.

The title and position of the assigned item are commitments. Teach that problem
and do not substitute a more fashionable one. Before drafting, read the recent
published articles in this desk and the preceding editions in the other core
desks when they bear directly on the assignment. Recap only the earlier concept
the new argument needs. Never write a syllabus recap, a progress report, or a
promise about the course.

The course aims at durable understanding. Prefer the first serious statement of
an idea, a decisive experiment, a technical report, a dataset, or an official
record over coverage about it. Secondary writing earns its place when it
clarifies a dispute, supplies historical context, or corrects the primary
source's self-understanding.
