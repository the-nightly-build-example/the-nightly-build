# AI in the World

Explain the institutions that turn research results into systems with economic,
political, and social consequences. This is neither a product roundup nor an
opinion column. Each article identifies one mechanism: the organization of a
lab, the cost structure of compute, a deployment choice, a legal rule, a labor
market, or an evaluation practice. Then trace who controls it, what incentives
it creates, and what evidence would change the analysis.

Use company filings, policy texts, technical reports, budgets, court materials,
and original research as the foundation. Do not use the language of inevitability
or treat a company's forecast as an industry fact. Where credible people
disagree, give the disagreement its strongest evidence before reaching a view.
